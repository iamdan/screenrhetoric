Blessing Critique

This video brings together a number of quotations and narration to discuss the subject of alternative scholarship. It also shows a performative response to a poem. The performative response contains several layers with images and live music. The transcript below captures the narration and performed response and key texts on screen.

00:00 Title screen: The Blessing of Rescuing Critique Daniel Anderson @iamdan. The video opens with the text of a quote on the screen (See aside). Imagery of wild horses appears in the lower portion of the video. The narrator speaks: In this piece, I show how multimedia invites us to move from concrete to abstract, opening avenues for emergent interpretation. I also experiment with a mode of response that recognizes some of the limits of critique and closure. Critique can be competitive, exclusionary, banal. Closure can lead us to overlook the generosity of the world around us, including the world of the texts we study.

[00:37] The text of the poem, "A Blessing" begins scrolling on the screen. The narrator speaks: Practicing a mode of alt-scholarship, I created a response to this poem scrolling on the screen, "A Blessing," by James Wright. The poem is set off a rural highway near Rochester, Minnesota. It is about two companions who stumble across a pair of horses in a meadow. The poem suggests a bond between human and animal and among humans, an emotional connection.

[01:04] The narrator continues speaking: This emotional connection is often lost among the logic of academic closure. But multimodal is well suite to convey affect. Can it also help us produce critical analyis that resists closure? The works we study are rich and ambiguous, connecting with all of our senses, unfolding like flowers. Why not our responses?

[01:28] The narrator continues: These kinds of responses can create new subjectivities. "A Blessing" depicts a coming together as the speaker and his companion connect with "two Indian ponies." And the poem extends that human-animal connection with a metaphor that transcends the subject/object divide and generates a new sense of self at one with the world. "Suddenly I realize / That if I stepped out of my body I would break / Into blossom."

About the Video

This video opens with segments that present explication and citation related to the subject of alternative scholarship. It then transitions into a critical response to the poem, "A Blessing," by James Wright. The narration and response to the poem were first performed at the 2016 Computers and Writing Conference. The response, however, was a remake of an earlier effort that used horse imagery rather than the motif of drag racing in this version. The switch from horse to racing imagery represents a move from the concrete to the abstract. Wright's poem deals directly with horses. The video that drifts further away from the direct link gains complexity through extra layers of meaning.
