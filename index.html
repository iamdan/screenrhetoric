Writing now includes images, sounds, and motion in spaces that are emergent, ephemeral, and networked. The video scholarship here responds to this moment. The response does not try to directly translate writing from print to screen. Instead, it highlights negative aspects of academic writing and experiments with alternative approaches. The result is scholarship that focuses on ways of knowing that are windowed, layered, and multimodal, pushes against print-centric expectations, and deliberately deploys ambiguity, affect, and creative production.

Screen composing draws from methods linked with creative production--from visual rhetoric to cinema to music to performance to the spoken word. It extends this history with affordances that are particular to the screen-- software, gestures, interfaces, and networked materials. As scholarship moves through the screen, print behaviors are brought into sharp relief, speculative approaches, and new methods emerge in the classroom. The videos collected here capture some of these possibilities.
